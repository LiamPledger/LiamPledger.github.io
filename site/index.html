Structural Earthquake Engineering

About

I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering with 1st Class Honors from the University of Canterbury and completed my PhD from 2022 to 2025, also at the University of Canterbury. My dissertation quantified how reducing design drift limits for structures influences the performance of the building and contents by (a) assimilating data from field reconnaissance (b) conducting large-scale dynamic tests of non-structural elements, and (c) performing nonlinear dynamic analyses of structures to estimate the collapse probability, upfront cost, and seismic loss.

I now work as a post-doctoral researcher at ETH Zurich within the chair of Structural Dynamics and Earthquake Engineering. My current research involves performing large-scale cyclic tests of squat T-shaped and L-shaped RC walls to quantify the behaviour and failure-modes of such walls and to evaluate modelling techniques for complex wall configurations.
